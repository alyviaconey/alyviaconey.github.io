The Carbon
Wallet Dashboard

A full-stack web application built to help clients track and manage their carbon credit investments. Developed during my internship at The Energy Consortium, this dashboard provides real-time portfolio monitoring, credit expiration tracking, and data-driven recommendations. Built with Python, Flask, and MySQL, the application handles millions of data points while offering interactive visualizations and secure user authentication to help clients make informed decisions about their carbon credit trades.

Colbourne Lab
DIICP & RICP Analysis

A Python-based analysis program developed to assist neuroscience research at the University of Alberta's Colbourne Lab. The program processes and analyzes intracranial pressure (ICP) data to predict critical brain pressure events (DIICP and RICP). This work contributed to published research examining stroke outcomes and ICP monitoring, advancing our understanding of acute stroke care. Game of Thrones
Data Story

A comprehensive data analysis and visualization project exploring character trends, mortality, and demographics in Game of Thrones. Using a meticulously hand-compiled dataset from published academic research, this analysis investigates character introduction patterns and gender representation across seasons, examining how the show's character dynamics evolved over time. The project delves into main character prominence, identifies the series' most tragic episodes through mortality patterns, analyzes the deadliest characters, and explores house survival rates throughout the series.

  • View Data Story

Alberta TIER
Data Analysis

A data visualization project built in Tableau that provides the project validation team of The Energy Consortium with clear insights into Alberta's carbon credit distribution. Using data from TIER (Technology Innovation and Emissions Reduction), I created interactive dashboards showing: rankings of the largest carbon credit holders, visualization of how company groupings affect market positions, breakdown of credit quantities across different companies, and alternative views for parent company relationships
